The 3 Main Types Of Chocolates

Dark Chocolate

Dark chocolate is a chocolate of about 65-99% cocoa mass and 1-40% sugar. What sets this chocolate apart from the others is that it does not contain any milk powder. Because of its potential low sugar content, dark chocolate is also said to have health benefits such as:

  • Lower risk of Diabetes
  • Important minerals including zinc, iron, and magnesium
  • High fiber
  • Improved blood flow
Dark Chocolate can vary on taste depending on sugar, sometimes tasting bitter or sweet.

History Of Dark Chocolate

Dark chocolate was invented by the ancient Mayans, who often used drank it with beverages and spices in ceremonies and other important events that were related to trade. Dark chocolate was also extremely common, with cocoa beans being the Mayan Civilizations' currency. Because of this, any family could have chocolate regardless of wealth or status.
